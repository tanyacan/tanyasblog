a few years ago, I came across Joan Didion's essay titled "Why I Write." while the specifics of the essay have faded from my memory, the title has always stuck with me. originally coined by Orwell, the title is obviously extremely catchy… but also a sad reminder of what I lack

navigating my relationship with writing has been a constant challenge. I find immense pleasure in immersing myself in the written works of others, considering it a profound form of intimacy. the power of words has consistently served as a source of solace, especially in moments where I struggle to articulate or process my emotions. exposure to the vulnerability captured by others served as a form of avoidance, hindering any motivation to express myself and my own feelings; I thought the inherent vulnerability in both the act of writing and the result of the product was something I could leech from others. but at the end of the day, I’m probably doing myself a great disservice..

with the start of the new year, I want to challenge myself by consistently processing and writing. I don’t expect these short journal entries to be anything spectacular, but I do hope to improve on these three specific aspects through this:

  • rediscovering what makes me passionate; dropping detachment
  • becoming confident in my voice and perspectives
  • owning my emotions
